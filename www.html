World Wide Web

The World Wide Web, commonly referred to as the web, is a system of interlinked hypertext documents and resources that are accessed via the internet. It was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 and has since become a fundamental part of modern life, enabling people to access information, communicate, and conduct business online.

The web operates on the principle of hyperlinks, allowing users to navigate between different web pages and resources by clicking on links. It is built on top of the internet infrastructure and relies on various protocols, such as HTTP (Hypertext Transfer Protocol) and HTTPS (HTTP Secure), to facilitate communication between web browsers and web servers.

The web has evolved significantly since its inception, with the introduction of new technologies and standards that have enhanced its functionality and user experience. It has transformed the way we access and share information, connect with others, and conduct commerce, making it an integral part of our daily lives.
