Web 1.0

Web 1.0, also known as the "Static Web," refers to the early stage of the World Wide Web, which emerged in the 1990s. During this period, websites were primarily static and consisted of simple HTML pages with limited interactivity. Web 1.0 was characterized by a one-way flow of information, where users could only consume content created by webmasters or organizations.

Web 1.0 websites were often basic and lacked dynamic features, such as user-generated content, social media integration, and interactive elements. The focus was on providing information rather than fostering user engagement or collaboration.
Web 1.0 laid the foundation for the development of the web, but it was limited in terms of functionality and user experience compared to later iterations like Web 2.0 and Web 3.0.
